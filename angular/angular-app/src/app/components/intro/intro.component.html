E91 Protocol

Proposed in 1991 by Artur Ekert, E91 protocol is an entanglement-based protocol which uses Bell states shared between Alice and Bob for secure distribution of secret key . ​



SHARING BELL STATES

Choose the sender and receiver from topology that was setup prior .The sender (Alice) and receiver (Bob) share n number of |ψ-⟩ bell states , where n depends on the length of the secret key to be generated.​


MEASURE AND RECORD RESULTS​


Alice makes a measurement with a direction randomly chosen between Horizontal (X observable),Diagonal (W observable),Vertical (Z observable). Whereas Bob makes a measurement with a direction randomly chosen between Diagonal (W observable),Vertical (Z observable), Anti Diagonal (V observable). Alice and Bob record the measurement results .


REVEALING THE BASES

Alice and Bob broadcast the measurement basis which they used, through the classical channel .If Alice and Bob have measured the spin projections of the entangled pair of qubits onto the same direction (i.e. W--W or Z--Z for Alice's and Bob's qubit respectively), then they are sure that they obtained opposite results. i.e. the results will be perfectly anticorrelated and thus get their key string.​​ ​


E2E Protocol


E2E (End to End entanglement) application is related to generate n-entangled pair of a target fidelity between two nodes, the source and the destination, those can be present anywhere in a given topology.



Request arising & Path finding

A source node will raise a request to generate n-entangled pair of a target fidelity between a destination node.​ Routing algorithm will choose the best path from the source to destination between all possible paths.​​


Resource allocation, entanglement generation , purification, Swapping​

After choosing the best path entanglement generation & will be completed in between all adjacent nodes. Entanglement swapping will be done in between intermediate nodes.​


End to End entanglement generation

After entanglement swapping between the intermediate nodes end to end entanglement will be done in between the source & destination node.​​​ ​


GHZ

GHZ (Greenberger–Horne–Zeilinger state )state is a three party non-classically correlated quantum state. This application is related to preparation of a three party entangled pair .​


CHOOSE SOURCE , DESTINATION AND CENTRAL NODES

Specify the three nodes (one source and two destinations) and one central node ( which is the approximately equidistant from three nodes and enough resources to get high fidelity entanglement) from the topology that was setup prior.​

TWO PARTY BELL STATES

Three end to end 2 party entanglements are shared between central node and other three nodes. ​​

GHZ MEASUREMENT

Now Central node performs the GHZ measurement and send the results over classical communication channel to the other three nodes involved. Based on received results all three parties perform Local corrective Pauli Operations and thus share GHZ state between them .​​

Teleportation Protocol


TEL( Teleportation) protocol is regarding sharing an arbitrary quantum state between two parties with the help of entangled pair shared between them.​

{{tel.tel1|uppercase}}

The sender (Alice) wants to send quantum information (qubit state |ψ⟩=α|0⟩+β|1⟩) to receiver (Bob). Due to No-cloning theorem Alice cannot simply make an exact copy of an unknown quantum state and give the copy to Bob. We can only copy classical states (not superpositions).To transfer a quantum bit, Alice and Bob must share an entangled qubit pair.​​


{{tel.tel2|uppercase}}​

Alice then performs a Hadamard operation followed by a CNOT gate between a qubit it wants to send/teleport and the entangled qubit at Alice’s end . And then she measures the qubits out, which is called Bell-state measurement and sends the measurement results to Bob over a classical communication channel.​​


{{tel.tel3|uppercase}}

After Bob receives the message and he applies a corrective unitary operations (based on the results data that he gets from Alice ) on his entangled part of the qubit. The updated qubit that bob has now is the qubit that was at Alice’s end initially. We call this teleportation because, at the end , Bob will have |ψ⟩ and Alice won't anymore.​ ​​
