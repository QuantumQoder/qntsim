E91 Protocol

Proposed in 1991 by Artur Ekert, E91 protocol is an entanglement-based protocol which uses Bell states shared between Alice and Bob for secure distribution of secret key . ​



SHARING BELL STATES

Choose the sender and receiver from topology that was setup prior .The sender (Alice) and receiver (Bob) share n number of |ψ-⟩ bell states , where n depends on the length of the secret key to be generated.​


MEASURE AND RECORD RESULTS​


Alice makes a measurement with a direction randomly chosen between Horizontal (X observable),Diagonal (W observable),Vertical (Z observable). Whereas Bob makes a measurement with a direction randomly chosen between Diagonal (W observable),Vertical (Z observable), Anti Diagonal (V observable). Alice and Bob record the measurement results .


REVEALING THE BASES

Alice and Bob broadcast the measurement basis which they used, through the classical channel .If Alice and Bob have measured the spin projections of the entangled pair of qubits onto the same direction (i.e. W--W or Z--Z for Alice's and Bob's qubit respectively), then they are sure that they obtained opposite results. i.e. the results will be perfectly anticorrelated and thus get their key string.​​ ​


E2E Protocol


E2E (End to End entanglement) application is related to generate n-entangled pair of a target fidelity between two nodes, the source and the destination, those can be present anywhere in a given topology.



{{e2e.e2e1|uppercase}}

A source node will raise a request to generate n-entangled pair of a target fidelity between a destination node.​ Routing algorithm will choose the best path from the source to destination between all possible paths.​​


{{e2e.e2e2|uppercase}}​

After choosing the best path entanglement generation & will be completed in between all adjacent nodes. Entanglement swapping will be done in between intermediate nodes.​


{{e2e.e2e3|uppercase}}

After entanglement swapping between the intermediate nodes end to end entanglement will be done in between the source & destination node.​​​ ​


GHZ

GHZ (Greenberger–Horne–Zeilinger state )state is a three party non-classically correlated quantum state. This application is related to preparation of a three party entangled pair .​


CHOOSE SOURCE , DESTINATION AND CENTRAL NODES

Specify the three nodes (one source and two destinations) and one central node ( which is the approximately equidistant from three nodes and enough resources to get high fidelity entanglement) from the topology that was setup prior.​

TWO PARTY BELL STATES

Three end to end 2 party entanglements are shared between central node and other three nodes. ​​

GHZ MEASUREMENT

Now Central node performs the GHZ measurement and send the results over classical communication channel to the other three nodes involved. Based on received results all three parties perform Local corrective Pauli Operations and thus share GHZ state between them .​​

Teleportation Protocol


TEL( Teleportation) protocol is regarding sharing an arbitrary quantum state between two parties with the help of entangled pair shared between them.​


{{tel.tel1|uppercase}}

The sender (Alice) wants to send quantum information (qubit state |ψ⟩=α|0⟩+β|1⟩) to receiver (Bob). Due to No-cloning theorem Alice cannot simply make an exact copy of an unknown quantum state and give the copy to Bob. We can only copy classical states (not superpositions).To transfer a quantum bit, Alice and Bob must share an entangled qubit pair.​​


{{tel.tel2|uppercase}}​

Alice then performs a Hadamard operation followed by a CNOT gate between a qubit it wants to send/teleport and the entangled qubit at Alice’s end . And then she measures the qubits out, which is called Bell-state measurement and sends the measurement results to Bob over a classical communication channel.​​


{{tel.tel3|uppercase}}

After Bob receives the message and he applies a corrective unitary operations (based on the results data that he gets from Alice ) on his entangled part of the qubit. The updated qubit that bob has now is the qubit that was at Alice’s end initially. We call this teleportation because, at the end , Bob will have |ψ⟩ and Alice won't anymore.​ ​​

QSDC1


A QKD scheme using EPR pairs with each EPR pair carrying two bits of key code. This protocol is the seminal work on QSDC by G.L. Long and X.S. Liu https://arxiv.org/pdf/quant-ph/0012056.pdf.​


Alice prepare the qubits

Alice converts the classical message in binary string and inserts check bits in even position.​ Then she prepares, N EPR pairs and encodes bit-pairs into EPR pairs by the above encoding rule.​ Finally, she separates one qubit from each EPR pair and creates two sequences of N qubits.

Transmission of encoded qubits

Alice sends Sequence 2 to Bob over the Quantum channel and informs Bob about the position of check bits via classical channel.​

Alice and Bob checks for security

Bob measure the check bits in computational (or Z) basis and and acknowledges Alice about his measurement. Alice now after measuring her set of check bits, compares the outcome with Bob and identifies for any Eavesdropping. ​​

Bob decodes Alice's message

Upon successful Eavesdropping check, Alice now sends the remaining qubits to Bob via the Quantum channel. Bob now undergoes Bell State measurement on the qubit pairs and retrieves the binary string corresponding to the actual message.​​​​

Single Photon Quantum Dialogue

his changes the state of the photons which results in it carrying the information to the receiver. The receiver then encodes their message through their unitary rotation and then, measures out the state of the photon. The measurement results,initial states and the initial messages of each party helps them to decode the message of the other. The implementation refers to the research article:https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1009-1963/15/7/005


ENCODING OF FIRST MESSAGE​

One of the parties prepares required number of batches of photons, each in random initial states among |0>, |1>, |+> and |->. Then, they encode their message into the photons by applying I and Y pauli gates for bit value '0' and '1', respectively, and send the photons to the next party.

SECURITY CHECK OF THE CHANNELS​


The receiver would select some random photons in order to measure and verify the integrity of the channel. They achieve this measuring random photons and sharing the measurement outcomes with the source of the photons. The photon source verifies the measurement result according to the photons' initial state and message encoded and decides to proceed if the error values lie with the threshold.

ENCODING OF SECOND MESSAGE AND DECODING

After the verification passes the authenticity check in step 2, the second partner (receiver) would encode their message in the same scheme as in step 1, and then measure out the photons. The receiver shares this measurement outcomes over the public channel with the sender. And, the sender shares the information on the initial state of the photons over the public channel. The sender uses the information of the initial state, their to-be conveyed message and the measurement outcomes to decode the receiver's message. Similarly, the receiver uses the measurement outcomes, their to-be conveyed message and the information on the initial states to decode the sender's message.

QSDC with Controlled Teleportation


Quantum Information of an unknown quantum state of a two-level particle is communicated from Alice to Bob using pre-shared entanglements and teleportation in presence of a supervisor Charlie. Alice encodes the secret message directly on her sequence of particle states and transmits them to Bob. The protocol developed by T. Gao, F L Yan and Z X Wang has a unique feature where communication between two sides depends on the agreement of a third side. https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1009-1963/14/5/006/meta



PREPARING SHARED EN​TANGLEMENT​

The protocol requires 3 parties to come together. For instance say, Charlie share a 3-qubit entanglement with Alice, Bob and himself.​ This entanglement can also be shared by someone like David where he prepares the entanglement and shares the qubits with Alice, Bob and Charlie.


ALICE ENCODES THE MESSAGE​


The protocol is performed by controlled teleportation meaning there is teleportation happening but in a controlled manner which ultimately enables the protocol execution. Alice now encodes the original classical messages with a pre-decided encoding scheme and performs appropriate transformation in her entangled set of qubits. Then she creates message qubits in accordance to the encoded sequence meaning the message qubit is prepared in |+> state if the encoded entangled qubit is also in |+> state.


MEASUREMENT AND DECODING

Now, once this preparation is completed, Charlie measures her entangled set of qubits in computational basis. He now communicates his result to Alice and Bob via a classical channel. This is the step where trust on Charlie is expected. With Charlie's measurement been done, Alice performs Joint Measurement (or Bell state measurement)​​ on her qubit pairs and communicates her result to Bob via Classical channel. Finally Bob uses this information from Alice and Charlie to perform corrective operations on her qubits to decode the original message that was been sent. ​
